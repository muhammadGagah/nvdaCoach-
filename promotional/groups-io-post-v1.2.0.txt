I wanted to share a new release of NVDA Coach and take a moment to properly thank

NVDA Coach is a free add-on for NVDA that teaches commands through guided,
step-by-step practice sessions from inside NVDA itself — no PDFs, no videos,
no switching contexts. Press NVDA+Shift+C and the Coach walks you through what
to do and why, one step at a time.

WHAT'S NEW IN V1.2.0
---------------------

1. Understanding Command Categories — new opening lesson in Chapter 1

This is the change I'm most proud of in this release, and it came directly from
a conversation in this community.

Brian made a point that stopped me in my tracks: rather than labeling individual
commands with tags like "[NVDA command]" or "[Universal shortcut]" on every step,
learners need to understand the framework once — clearly, early, and conceptually.

There are three categories:

  Windows commands — handled by the operating system. Cut, Copy, Paste, and opening
  the Start Menu are all Windows operations. Many programs call Windows to perform
  these; the programs themselves are not doing the work.

  Program commands — handled by the application. Pressing Ctrl+N in Word opens a new
  document. Pressing the down arrow moves the cursor in the program. The screen reader
  reads what you moved to, but the *program* moved the cursor.

  Screen reader commands — handled by NVDA. These take no action in the program.
  They only announce, read, or describe. NVDA+T announces the title bar — nothing
  changes, you just find out where you are. A sighted person can glance at the title
  bar; a blind person cannot, so NVDA supplies the equivalent.

That last distinction — "it takes no action, it only informs" — is something Brian
articulated better than I had in two years of writing this material. It's now Lesson 1.

All the inline labels from v1.1.0 have been removed (28 instances across all five
lesson files). The framework lesson does that work instead. We're trusting learners
to carry the concept forward on their own.

2. Sound branding overhaul

All five sound moments in the Coach — lesson start, step advance, hint, lesson stop,
and lesson complete — now use custom audio files instead of generated beeps. The
sound toggle in NVDA Settings (NVDA menu > Preferences > Settings > NVDA Coach)
disables everything if you prefer silence.

3. NVDA Coach Help in the NVDA Help menu

NVDA menu > Help > NVDA Coach Help now opens the user guide directly.
